Itinerary

Starting my journey from Santa Barbara, where I was living at the time during my studies at UCSB, the drive to Death Valley took around 6 hours. But oh, what an adventure it was! Every mile brought new sights that filled my heart with joy and wonder.

In Death Valley, Artist Palette's colorful hills looked like they were from a dream. At Dante View, I saw the vastness of Death Valley spread out before me, it was so peaceful. I visited Badwater Basin, the lowest point in North America, where the salt flats stretched endlessly. Despite the heat, I walked on them, feeling the crunchy salt beneath my feet. And the Mesquite Sand Dunes? They felt like a never ending sea of sand. It was like being in a desert paradise. Make sure to stay hydrated and pack snacks for the journey since there is not much food around! And of course, I took lots of photos to remember the trip.

If you ever want an adventure that'll leave you speechless, I recommend Death Valley. Just check the weather and road conditions first, I recommend winter and spring so it doesn't get too hot. Get ready for an unforgettable journey.
